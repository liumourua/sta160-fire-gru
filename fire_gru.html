Short-term ignition (GRU) and post-fire hazard analysis

Methods (summary)

We model daily wildfire ignition on a 10–15 km grid using a GRU with a learned spatial ignition prior and history-safe meteorological covariates. The GRU predicts the probability that at least one new fire occurs in the next 7 or 14 days, with strict time splits between training (2013–2021), validation (2022), and test (2023–2025).

For post-fire hazard, we merge ignitions within 3 days into a single episode and compute waiting times between episodes for each site. A discrete-time hazard model provides regional estimates of median inter-fire gaps and the probability of re-ignition within 30 or 60 days.
